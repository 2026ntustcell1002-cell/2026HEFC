About HEFC 2026

Conference Info

Date

Venue
International Building, NTUST

Purpose and Vision

Hydrogen energy and fuel cell technologies are critical elements in global pathways toward net-zero emissions. This conference aims to integrate frontier academic research with industrial commercialization practices, focusing on technical bottlenecks, academia-industry collaboration, and intelligent energy transition.

Through this gathering, we hope to build consensus across government, industry, academia, and research communities, and jointly advance the practical implementation of hydrogen technologies. HEFC 2026 emphasizes technology deployment and international connection, inviting leading scholars and industry experts to discuss the future of the hydrogen supply chain.

Conference Goals

  • Technical ExchangePromote academic and applied exchange in hydrogen energy and fuel cell technologies.
  • Academia-Industry MatchingConnect academic research capabilities with industrial technology needs.
  • Talent DevelopmentEncourage young researchers and students to engage with green energy issues.
  • Policy DialogueProvide a dialogue platform among policymakers, academia, and industry.

Core Topics

Hydrogen Energy

Hydrogen production, purification, storage, water electrolysis, and power-to-gas (P2G) technologies.

Fuel Cells

Low-temperature and high-temperature fuel cell materials and systems.

Hydrogen Fuel Systems

Hydrogen supply, storage and transport management, and energy application technologies.
